Each Malaysian voter can only casts one vote to elect one Member of Parliament (MP) but under the current electoral system, not every vote has the same power. For example, in a thinly-populated constituency like Putrajaya, the 17,925 voters there vote to elect one MP while in a over-crowded constituency like Subang, 130,211 voters - seven times the number of voters in Putrajaya - also elect one MP.

In other words, one Putrajaya vote is equal to seven Subang vote. Subang voters are under-represented while Putrajaya voter is over-represented. This malpractice is called malapportionment, a violation of the “one person, one vote, one value” principle in a democratic political system.
